## Tier 1 — Primary Targets

### AI Engineer World's Fair
- **Location:** San Francisco, CA, USA
- **Typical date:** June (annual)
- **CFP opens:** January–February
- **CFP deadline:** ~March 31
- **Format:** 30-min talks, workshops, demos
- **Audience:** AI engineers, builders, founders
- **Fit:** EXCELLENT — spatial AI UX is highly relevant; strong technical audience
- **Submission talk:** "Beyond Linear Chat: Designing Spatial AI Interfaces"
- **Notes:** Historically sells out; apply for speaker slot ASAP

### Svelte Summit
- **Location:** Online (occasionally hybrid)
- **Typical date:** Spring & Fall editions
- **CFP deadline:** Usually 8 weeks before event
- **Format:** 20–30 min talks
- **Audience:** Svelte developers, framework enthusiasts
- **Fit:** EXCELLENT — Træk is built on Svelte 5; perfect venue for the runes talk
- **Submission talk:** "Svelte 5 Runes in the Wild: Building a Real-Time Canvas Library"
- **Notes:** Svelte team is active here; great for ecosystem visibility

### ViteConf
- **Location:** Online (global)
- **Typical date:** October
- **CFP deadline:** ~July
- **Format:** 20 min talks
- **Audience:** Vite users, frontend ecosystem builders
- **Fit:** HIGH — SvelteKit/Vite users are a core Træk audience
- **Submission talk:** Either talk works; Svelte 5 runes talk preferred
- **Notes:** Large online audience; recordings get many views

## Notes on CFP Strategy

1. **Lead with the demo.** Every CFP submission should link to a 2-min demo video showing the canvas in action.
2. **Adapt abstracts per audience.** The React Summit version of "Beyond Linear Chat" should mention `@traek/react` explicitly.
3. **Speaker bio consistency.** Keep a canonical speaker bio in `/docs/devrel/speaker-bio.md`.
4. **Track acceptance rate.** Target 40%+ acceptance rate by tailoring talks to conference fit scores.
5. **Submit early.** Early submissions have historically better acceptance rates at most JS conferences.
